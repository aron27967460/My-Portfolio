Elevating the Financial Management Experience

As of 2024, almost half of Americans reported having diffculty to pay their bills. This is why EarnIn aims to reimagine the way money moves, so every person can better meet their financial potential. As their platform continued to pioneer as one of the most popular tools to access people's future earnings, they wanted to further elevate their in-app and user experience. Daylight's primary objective was to achieve that vision by venturing abundance of interaction patterns and product ideas.

ROLES

UX Designer

CLIENT

EarnIn

DURATION

Jan, 2024 - May, 2024

Introduction

One of the most fascinating aspects of working as a consultant is the opportunity to collaborate with clients from various industries. This diversity requires us to quickly learn and adapt, but at the same time, we got to leverage our outsider perspective to explore novel ideas and challenge our creative boundaries.

While working with our client from the financial services, we needed to understand concept of Earned Wage Access (EWA), as well as the functionality of their mobile app, the design system, and the ecosystem in which they operated in. Despite a rapid schedule of sprints, we managed to deliver innovative, high-fidelity designs with precision. We achieved this by exploring an abundance of ideas and rapid prototyping.

To achieve the best results, we often explore a wide range of ideas.

Navigation and Explore

As EarnIn continues to grow, more products are destined to come. With the ever-expanding list of features, EarnIn would like to re-think their navigation and discovery with the goal of simplified yet delightful cross-selling experience.

EXPLORATION

With a combination of the use of colour and interaction design, we explored a wide range of ideas of how users could access these features.

REFINEMENT

By reorganizing the bottom navigation bar, we designed a dynamic cross-selling experience to highlight commonly used products while enabling users to explore new or unused products from EarnIn—creating a smooth transition between the horizontal and vertical axes.

Onboarding

While functionality is important, EarnIn believes that a compelling user experience creates a long lasting relationship with the customers. The revamp of the onboarding experience was part of the initiative in making that vision come true.

EXPLORATION

From videos, illustrations, to wireframes, we explored ways to introduce EarnIn to the users in an engaging manner.

INTERACTIVE INTRO

From our ad-hoc user test, we learned that videos were the most engaging methods. However, the experience should be brief and short (e,g, instagram’s stories as example). Users should also be in formed on how long the experience may be and have control to skip or not.

While the video and copy were purely placeholder, the onboarding content should prioritize on explaining the different product benefit and value prop in simple language.

After the intro videos, we designed the account set up flow to be more aligned with EarnIn's brand with a progressive backdrop. We also connect that to a product selecting experience.

A branded account set up experience leading to select product setup.

Bill Reminder

From one of the sprints, the challenge we were tasked with is to create a customer's financial managing experience and help them better manage their finances. Considering usability factors such as spending categories and time spanning past and future, we worked with our client to craft a feature experience that is both functional and captivating.

EXPLORATION

OVERVIEW

From the client, we learned that customers often have a certain amount of recurring earnings and expenses that can be projected. Our goal was to visualize this information and showcase how their live income could cover their expenses due in the upcoming weeks. We aimed to create an interface that can communicate the relationship between these entities and their associated information. In addition, we recognized that not all bills have the same priority level, so we introduced another ordering mechanism that allows users to label their bills and see how prioritized bills are being covered compared to covering them by due dates.

A sankey diagram inspired visualization to showcase the relationship between one's earning and expenses. Users can also switch view between due dates and bill priority.

BILL DETAIL

While these bill details can be automatically determined by connecting to user's bank activity, we ensured they could also be manually adjusted. On top of that, we provide relevant actions such as setting notification before due dates or add to their calendar.

Users can click on individaul bill to edit details and access relevant actions.

PRODUCT SETUP

As part of the deliverables, we also designed the onboarding experience for users to set up their bill reminder. By connecting and analyzing their bank account, a list of recurring bills will populate. Through machine learning, these bills will be labelled into different priority (e.g., essential, important, secondary).

Overview to how users set up their bill reminder.

GOAL SETTING

A unique part of the onboarding for bill reminder was goal setting. Working with the client, we identified a set of saving goals with a duration filter that's based on past data. The core idea is to help EarnIn users to establish a realistic goal within reach.

Set up a realistic saving goal.

Impact

Over the course of project, we delivered several features and UX revamp. While the project was originally set to be 3 months, the client was extremely impressed with our performance and decided to extend the contract and continue our partnership. In fact, from one of the executives, he mentioned they "have to pause the collaboration because our development team can't keep with the amount of creativity you guys brought."
